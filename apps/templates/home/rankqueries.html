How query ranking works

The ranking is designed to highlight the queries most likely to deserve investigation first, based on the workload currently observed in the database.

The score is relative, not absolute. A query marked Critical or High is considered important compared with other queries in the same workload snapshot. On a lightly used database, these levels may be less meaningful.

Main factors used in the score

  • Total workload share: queries consuming the largest share of total execution time are prioritized.
  • Mean execution time: slower queries get a higher score.
  • Call volume share: frequently executed queries are ranked higher because their cumulative impact can be significant.
  • I/O share: queries responsible for a large share of block reads are considered more expensive.

Additional penalty signals

  • Poor cache efficiency: a low cache hit ratio increases the score.
  • Temp file usage: queries writing temp blocks are penalized.
  • Execution instability: highly variable execution time may increase the score.

Priority levels

  • Critical: highest-priority queries based on the current workload.
  • High: strong candidates for investigation.
  • Medium: worth reviewing if you are optimizing broader workload efficiency.
  • Low: currently less important than other queries in this ranking.

Important note

The ranking is a prioritization aid, not a verdict. A query can be frequent and harmless, or slow but rarely executed. Always interpret the score together with the query text, execution pattern, and business context.
